What is Demystify?

Technology can sometimes be intimidating. It's common to treat computers, networks or vehicles as black boxes, turn knobs and hope for the best. It doesn't have to be that way.

demystify is an effort to make learning how technology works more accessible. And as a term, its definition isn't really limited to the current wave of digital technologies.

demystify is taking shape as a camping festival near Bangalore and is a highly participative event.

As a participant, you'll pick something you're interested in ‚Äî radios, wood working or beer brewing ‚Äî learn how it's put together from peers, and then make it yourself. It's hands-on learning and experimentation at its best.

Take a workshop on weaving hats and lamp shades from the local crafts center, learn how to work with wood and bamboo or understand how you can setup a network in the country side. Make something new.

revive repair culture

Manufacturers are increasingly making it hard to repair our devices. Buying newer devices instead of repairing existing ones increases shareholder value. And also, e-waste piles. What if we can just repair what's broken?

At demystify, you'll learn how you can carry out basic home, electronics and vehicle repairs by yourself. It's not that hard and it's so satisfying when something broken kicks back to life.

What are you interested in repairing? What can you help repair?

Reduce. Reuse. Recycle.

Bangalore is under fairly serious water stress. It's easy to complain about garbage piles around the city, but we're part of the problem.

At demystify, you'll learn and be inspired from people about how they're composting waste, conserving water and recycling products.

What are you interested in learning with respect to sustainibility? What can you share with the audience at demystify?
