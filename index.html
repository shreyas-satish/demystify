What is Demystify?

demystify is an effort to understand how and why things are made. It's taking shape as a camping festival near Bangalore and is a highly participative event. The agenda is largely informed by what the participants want to explore.

Making from scratch. Demystified.

Making things from scratch can be a whole lot more fun than just, you know, buying them. Especially when we collab with other fun people!

If you had three days to disconnect from the routine and try something new, what would you be interested in making? What can you help others learn how to make?

We asked this on Twitter recently and here are some responses for inspiration.

Explore the ethical issues around making

While making things is fun, it's just as important to understand the historical context and the unintended consequences of pushing things out into the world.

We don't make in isolation. Whatever we make affects people and the environment in a myriad ways; the social and power dynamics between people; our personal identities, privacy, agency and freedom.

With this mind, what topics would you like to explore? Anything you'd like to present a session on?

revive repair culture

Our devices are increasingly becoming hostile to repair. If we don't push back, the culture of prying things open to understand how they work and repair them, will simply become elusive.

What are you interested in repairing? What can you help repair?

Reuse. Recycle. Upcycle.

The environmental changes on our planet mean we're going to have to find more sustainable ways of living.

What are you interested in learning with respect to sustainibility? What can you share with the audience at demystify?

Set and setting

Start your mornings with coffee (or chai) and explore the hills through long hikes. Cook lunch together and grill barbecue at night. Listen to and sing along with some amazing musicians around campfires.

Set amid hills near Bangalore, it's the perfect escape from the urban routine. Making, art, nature and friendly company. What will you make of it?

Guidelines at camp

  • Respect personal space

    demystify is meant to be a safe and inclusive space. Always ask before engaging with someone's personal space. Respect their decision. Demystify will have awareness and child-care teams on site.

  • Care for the environment

    Do not bring any plastic that will be disposed on site. Leave the place better than it was.

  • Engage

    demystify is a highly participative event. Make something, present a session, engage in discussions or volunteer and help put an amazing event together. Passive participation is highly discouraged.

  • Make it our own

    demystify is what we make of it. We live together for a weekend, look out for each other and treat camp like home.
